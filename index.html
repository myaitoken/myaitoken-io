The Challenge

The AI industry faces critical infrastructure bottlenecks that limit innovation and accessibility:

  • Prohibitive Costs: Cloud providers charge $2-15+/hour for GPU compute. H100 instances reach $30-50/hour, making AI development expensive for startups and researchers.
  • Severe Supply Constraints: High-end GPUs face 3-12 month lead times. NVIDIA H100/H200 waitlists extend into 2027 for many customers.
  • Centralization Risks: Oligopoly of AWS, Google Cloud, Azure controls 65%+ of cloud GPU market, creating single points of failure, privacy concerns, and censorship risks.
  • Exploding Demand: AI compute requirements double every 4-6 months. GPT-4 training required 25,000+ A100 GPUs. Future models will need exponentially more.
  • Underutilized Resources: Millions of GPUs sit idle in gaming PCs, crypto mining rigs, and enterprise data centers while developers face shortages.
  • Geographic Limitations: Centralized data centers create latency issues for edge AI applications and limit access in emerging markets.
